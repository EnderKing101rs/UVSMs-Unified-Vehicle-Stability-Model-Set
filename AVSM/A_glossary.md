AVSM Glossary: Air Vehicle Stability Model
The AVSM calculates the Stability Index (S) for aerial systems. It treats the atmosphere as a fluid medium where stability is a balance between lift-generated restoration and aerodynamic failure points like stalls or structural flutter.
The Master Equation (Air)

$$S_{Air} = \underbrace{\left[ \frac{L \cos\phi}{mg} + C_{l_\beta}\beta \right]}_{\text{Restoring}} + \underbrace{\left[ \eta \left( \frac{C_{L_{max}} - C_L}{C_{L_{max}}} \right) \right]}_{\text{Buffer}} - \underbrace{\left[ \sin\phi + \frac{F_{gust}}{mg} \right]}_{\text{Toppling}} - \underbrace{k \left[ \frac{\Delta x_{CG} + \tau \dot{\Delta x}}{L_{ref}} \right]}_{\text{Penalty}} - \underbrace{\left[ \frac{K_{wing} \gamma^2}{2mg L_{ref}} \right]}_{\text{Shock}}$$

Variable Definitions
1. Lift Restoring (The Machine's Will to Fly)
L: Lift force. The upward force generated by the wings.
\phi: Bank Angle. The degree of roll relative to the horizon.
C_{l_\beta}\beta: Dihedral Effect. The natural aerodynamic tendency of the wings to level themselves when slipping.
mg: Vehicle Weight. The product of mass and gravity that lift must overcome.
2. Stall Buffer (The Grip on the Air)
\eta: Air Fidelity. A multiplier (0 to 1) accounting for turbulence or wake.
C_{L_{max}}: Stall Limit. The maximum lift coefficient before the air "detaches" from the wing.
C_L: Current Lift Coefficient. The amount of "air-grip" currently being used.
3. Toppling Forces (The Aerial Environment)
F_{gust}: Wind Shear/Gust Force. Vertical or lateral air movement attempting to upset the frame.
4. Shift Penalty (The Pilot/Payload Factor)
\Delta x_{CG}: Center of Gravity Migration. The physical movement of passengers or cargo (Frame B) relative to the aircraft's neutral point (Frame A).
\tau: Pilot Reaction Lag. The delay in stick input to correct a pitch or roll deviation.
L_{ref}: Reference Length. Usually the wing chord or span, used to scale the weight shift.
5. Aeroelastic Shock (The Structural Limit)
K_{wing}: Wing Spar Rigidity. The stiffness of the aircraft's primary structural frame.
\gamma: Wing Deflection. The physical flex (flutter) caused by high-G maneuvers or turbulence.
